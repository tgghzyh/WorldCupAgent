The '''2026 FIFA World Cup{{efn-ua|Also marketed as '''FIFA World Cup 26'''<ref>{{cite web |url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/3567360896991b48/original/FIFA-World-Cup-26-IP-Guidelines.pdf |title=FIFA Intellectual Property Guidelines |publisher=[[FIFA]] |date=June 2024 |access-date=April 28, 2026 |archive-date=April 24, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260424033458/https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/3567360896991b48/original/FIFA-World-Cup-26-IP-Guidelines.pdf |url-status=live }}</ref> and '''FIFA World Cup 2026'''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026 |title=Official website at FIFA.com |publisher=[[FIFA]] |date= |access-date=June 17, 2026 |archive-date=June 16, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260616215435/https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026 |url-status=live }}</ref>}}''' is the 23rd and current [[FIFA World Cup]], the [[quadrennial]] international men's [[soccer]]<!--Per WP:ENGVAR, DO NOT change the reference to "football." Consensus is to use "soccer," consistent with other North American articles, as that is the name used in two of the three host countries for this tournament.--> championship contested by the [[List of men's national association football teams|national teams]] of the member associations of [[FIFA]]. The tournament began on June 11, 2026, and will conclude on July 19.<ref name="Schedule" /> It is jointly hosted by 16 cities{{snd}}11 in the United States, 3 in Mexico, and 2 in Canada. The tournament is the first FIFA World Cup to be hosted by three nations and the first to include 48 teams, an expansion from the previous 32-team format.<ref>{{Cite news |date=2026-06-24 |title=FIFA World Cup 2026: Five nations bow out as race for round of 32 heats up |url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/sports/football/fifa-world-cup/fifa-world-cup-2026-five-nations-bow-out-as-race-for-round-of-32-heats-up/articleshow/131960169.cms |access-date=2026-06-26 |work=The Times of India |issn=0971-8257}}</ref>

It is the first World Cup since [[2002 FIFA World Cup|2002]] to be hosted by multiple nations. With its past hosting of the [[1970 FIFA World Cup|1970]] and [[1986 FIFA World Cup|1986]] tournaments, Mexico became the first country to host or co-host the World Cup three times.<ref name=":04">{{Cite web |title=Qualified teams for the FIFA World Cup 2026 |url=https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/world-cup-2026-who-has-qualified |access-date=2026-06-26 |website=www.fifa.com |lang=en |archive-date=June 11, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260611195321/https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/world-cup-2026-who-has-qualified |url-status=live }}</ref> The United States previously hosted the [[1994 FIFA World Cup|World Cup in 1994]]. By contrast, it is Canada's first time hosting or co-hosting the tournament.<ref name=":04" /> The event returned to its traditional June–July schedule after the [[2022 FIFA World Cup|2022 World Cup]] in Qatar, which was uniquely held in November and December to avoid Qatar's extreme summer heat.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-11-19 |title=Why is the World Cup being played in November, not in Qatar's summer? {{!}} Sporting News |url=https://www.sportingnews.com/us/soccer/news/Qatar-+World-Cup-November-Winter-Summer/lbimjdtaysxdxbuuxp30btt5 |access-date=2026-07-03 |website=www.sportingnews.com |language=en-us}}</ref>

As host nations, [[Canada men's national soccer team|Canada]], [[Mexico national football team|Mexico]], and the [[United States men's national soccer team|United States]] all automatically qualified. [[Cape Verde national football team|Cape Verde]], [[Curaçao national football team|Curaçao]], [[Jordan national football team|Jordan]], and [[Uzbekistan national football team|Uzbekistan]] all made their World Cup debuts.<ref name=":04" /> [[Argentina national football team|Argentina]] is the [[2022 FIFA World Cup final|defending champion]], having won its third World Cup in 2022. The tournament set the record for the [[FIFA World Cup records and statistics#Attendance|highest attendance in World Cup history]]. On June 25, 2026, total attendance reached 3,605,357 spectators, surpassing the previous record held for 32 years by the American-hosted [[1994 FIFA World Cup]].<ref name="USATodayRecord2026" />

===New rules===
The 2026 FIFA World Cup introduces several [[Laws of the Game (association football)|rule]] changes. In accordance with [[International Football Association Board|IFAB]], these are primarily designed to reduce [[Running out the clock|time-wasting]]. The new rules for the tournament include:<ref name="newrules">{{cite news |last1=Sanjesh Singh |title=What are the new rules for 2026 World Cup? Covering mouths, making subs and more |url=https://www.nbcnewyork.com/world-cup/new-rules-covering-mouths-substitutions-breaks/6510949/ |access-date=June 10, 2026 |publisher=NBC New York |date=June 9, 2026}}</ref>
* 10-second substitutions: Players being [[Substitute (association football)|substituted]] have 10 seconds to exit the field, otherwise the substitute must wait for one minute before entering the match.
* 5-second restarts: A visual 5-second countdown can be shown by the referee for throw-ins and goal kicks in situations of time-wasting. If the ball is not put into play in time, a restart is awarded to the opposing team; a [[corner kick]] is awarded if the infraction occurs pending a goal kick.
* Medical treatment: Any outfield player who receives medical attention on the field must leave the field and wait for 1 minute before returning to play.
* Expanded [[video assistant referee]] (VAR): VAR can now review and overturn clear mistakes on red cards given from a second yellow card, wrongly awarded corner kicks, and certain attacking fouls.
* Mouth-covering red cards: To stop confrontational or insulting behavior hidden from [[lip-reading]], any player who covers his mouth with his hand, arm, or shirt while confronting an opponent is to be [[Red card (soccer)|sent off]].
* Player leaving the field in protest: Players or officials who leave the field in protest are to be sent off.

The tiebreakers for when teams finish the group stage equal on points also changed. The first tiebreaker is now the number of points earned in head-to-head matches between the tied teams, not goal difference among all matches.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/groups-how-teams-qualify-tie-breakers |access-date=June 20, 2026 |website=FIFA |title=World Cup 2026 groups: How teams qualify and tie-breakers |archive-date=June 19, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260619142350/https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/groups-how-teams-qualify-tie-breakers |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last1=Mendola |first1=Nicholas |last2=Acosta |first2=Amelia |date=June 5, 2026 |title=What are the new group stage tiebreaker rules for the 2026 World Cup? |url=https://www.nbcsports.com/soccer/news/what-are-the-new-group-stage-tiebreaker-rules-at-the-2026-world-cup |access-date=June 20, 2026 |website=NBC Sports |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Allen |first=William |date=November 26, 2022 |title=What tiebreakers are used in the Qatar World Cup 2022 group stage? |url=https://en.as.com/soccer/what-tiebreakers-are-used-in-the-qatar-world-cup-2022-group-stage-n/ |access-date=June 20, 2026 |website=AS USA |language=en-us |archive-date=January 24, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260124032429/https://en.as.com/soccer/what-tiebreakers-are-used-in-the-qatar-world-cup-2022-group-stage-n/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

===Breaks===
Cooling or [[hydration break]]s were introduced at the [[2014 World Cup]] in Brazil. For the 2026 World Cup, FIFA introduced mandatory 3-minute hydration [[time-out (sport)|breaks in every half for all matches]]. Broadcasters are permitted to run [[Television timeout|commercials]] during these pauses.<ref name="Breaks">{{cite news |title=Players to benefit from hydration breaks at FIFA World Cup 2026 |url=https://inside.fifa.com/organisation/news/hydration-breaks-world-cup-2026-player-welfare |access-date=June 11, 2026 |publisher=FIFA |date=December 7, 2025 |archive-date=June 10, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260610235941/https://inside.fifa.com/organisation/news/hydration-breaks-world-cup-2026-player-welfare |url-status=live }}</ref>

The match schedule, without group assignments, was announced on February 4, 2024.<ref name="Schedule" /><ref name=":6">{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/media-releases/fifa-world-cup-26-tm-final-to-be-held-in-new-york-new-jersey-mexico-city-to |title=FIFA World Cup 26 final to be held in New York New Jersey, Mexico City to host historic opening match as schedule revealed |publisher=FIFA |date=February 4, 2024 |access-date=February 4, 2024 |archive-date=February 7, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240207073146/https://www.fifa.com/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/media-releases/fifa-world-cup-26-tm-final-to-be-held-in-new-york-new-jersey-mexico-city-to |url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="Q&A">{{cite web |url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/23aa99b288fe8a0d/original/FWC26-Match-Schedule-Q-A.pdf |title=FIFA World Cup 26 match schedule Q&A |publisher=FIFA |date=February 4, 2024 |access-date=February 4, 2024 |archive-date=February 5, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240205034420/https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/23aa99b288fe8a0d/original/FWC26-Match-Schedule-Q-A.pdf |url-status=live}}</ref> On June 13, 2024, FIFA released an updated schedule, with specific pairings assigned to venues for the knockout stage.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://inside.fifa.com/about-fifa/organisation/news/fifa-world-cup-26-shares-teams-homes-away-from-home-host-nations |title=FIFA World Cup 26 shares teams' "homes away from home"; host nations' potential pathways to glory unveiled |publisher=FIFA |date=June 12, 2024 |access-date=June 14, 2024 |archive-date=July 12, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240712083324/https://inside.fifa.com/about-fifa/organisation/news/fifa-world-cup-26-shares-teams-homes-away-from-home-host-nations |url-status=live}}</ref> In addition, group stage matches were assigned to specific groups (though pairings for non-host groups were not assigned to specific matches until after the final draw; thus the group venues were known, but not for which specific pairing each matchday). The full schedule was unveiled in a live broadcast on December 6, 2025, the day after the draw. Group stage pairings were allocated to specific matches, and the kickoff times were confirmed for all fixtures.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/updated-match-schedule-live-in-washington-dc-6-december |title=Updated match schedule to be unveiled live in Washington DC on 6 December |publisher=[[FIFA]] |date=December 1, 2025 |access-date=December 1, 2025}}</ref>

The final match will feature the first-ever [[Super Bowl]]-style [[2026 FIFA World Cup final halftime show|halftime show]] in FIFA World Cup history. Produced by Global Citizen and curated by [[Chris Martin]] of [[Coldplay]], the performance will co-headline [[Madonna]], [[Shakira]], and [[BTS]] to support the FIFA Global Citizen Education Fund.<ref>{{cite web |date=May 14, 2026 |title=FIFA announces Super Bowl-style World Cup final halftime show featuring Madonna, Shakira and BTS |url=https://apnews.com/article/world-cup-final-halftime-show-f08a3cc88e5c1dfccf0517941458df2f |website=AP News |access-date=June 11, 2026}}</ref>

The opening match was announced to include Mexico; it took place on June 11, 2026, at the [[Estadio Azteca]] in [[Mexico City]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Bosher |first=Luke |date=December 6, 2025 |title=Mexico drawn in 2026 World Cup Group A: What to know about South Africa, South Korea, playoff teams |url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/6854677/2025/12/05/world-cup-draw-mexico-group-schedule-fixtures/ |access-date=April 21, 2026 |work=The New York Times |issn=0362-4331 |archive-date=December 5, 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20251205211928/https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/6854677/2025/12/05/world-cup-draw-mexico-group-schedule-fixtures/ |url-status=live }}</ref> The opening match involving Canada took place on June 12 at [[BMO Field]] in [[Toronto]], while the United States' opening match took place on the same day at [[SoFi Stadium]] in [[Inglewood, California|Inglewood]]. Each host nation played each of its three matches in the group stage within its own country.<ref name=":6" />

[[AT&T Stadium]] in [[Arlington, Texas|Arlington]] hosts the most matches of any venue at the tournament, with nine. The United States host 78 matches, including all quarterfinals, semifinals, and the [[2026 FIFA World Cup final|final]] to be played at [[MetLife Stadium]] in [[East Rutherford, New Jersey|East Rutherford]] on July 19. Canada and Mexico each host 13 matches. Each tournament venue, except for the [[Estadio Akron]], host at least one knockout stage fixture.<ref>{{cite web |last=Dominski |first=Michael |url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/live-blogs/world-cup-2026-schedule-announcement-live-updates-final/r7oDr4oG9qK4/ |title=World Cup 2026 schedule announcement live updates: Latest as FIFA selects host city for final |publisher=[[The Athletic]] |date=February 4, 2024 |access-date=February 4, 2024 |archive-date=February 6, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240206103527/https://theathletic.com/live-blogs/world-cup-2026-schedule-announcement-live-updates-final/r7oDr4oG9qK4/ |url-status=live}}</ref> The match schedule overlaps with the [[2026 CFL season]], resulting in scheduling conflicts and loss of home games for the [[Toronto Argonauts]] and [[BC Lions]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://3downnation.com/2025/08/15/toronto-argonauts-to-play-three-extra-away-games-during-2026-fifa-world-cup/ |title=Toronto Argonauts to play three extra away games during 2026 FIFA World Cup |date=August 15, 2025 |website=3DownNation |archive-date=August 20, 2025 |access-date=August 18, 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250820010245/https://3downnation.com/2025/08/15/toronto-argonauts-to-play-three-extra-away-games-during-2026-fifa-world-cup/ |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://3downnation.com/2025/05/29/b-c-lions-weighing-different-markets-temporary-stadium-for-2026-world-cup-eviction/ |title=B.C. Lions weighing different markets, temporary stadium for 2026 World Cup eviction |date=May 29, 2025 |website=3DownNation |first=JC |last=Abbott}}</ref> The match schedule also affects the [[2026 Major League Baseball season]] schedules of the [[Kansas City Royals]], [[Philadelphia Phillies]], [[Seattle Mariners]], and [[Texas Rangers (baseball)|Texas Rangers]], whose home stadiums are located near World Cup venues.<ref>{{Cite web |date=August 26, 2025 |title=MLB announces 2026 regular season schedule |url=https://www.mlb.com/press-release/press-release-mlb-announces-2026-regular-season-schedule |website=MLB}}</ref>

==Host selection==
{{Main|2026 FIFA World Cup bids}}
The [[United 2026 FIFA World Cup bid|United 2026 bid]] beat a [[Morocco 2026 FIFA World Cup bid|rival bid by Morocco]] during a final vote at the [[68th FIFA Congress]] in [[Moscow]].

During the bidding process, 41 cities with 42 existing, fully functional venues with regular tenants (except [[Olympic Stadium (Montreal)|Olympic Stadium]] in [[Montreal]]) and two venues then under construction ([[Allegiant Stadium]] in [[Las Vegas]] and [[SoFi Stadium]] in [[Los Angeles]]) submitted to be part of the bid: three venues in three cities in Mexico, six venues in six cities in Canada, and 35 venues in 32 cities in the United States.<ref name="bids">{{cite web |title=Forty-one Cities Across Canada, Mexico and the United States Submit Bids to Serve as Host Cities in United Bid for 2026 FIFA World Cup |url=http://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2017/09/07/18/05/20170907-news-wc2026-41-cities-canada-mexico-usa-submit-bids-as-2026-world-cup-hosts |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170908021347/http://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2017/09/07/18/05/20170907-news-wc2026-41-cities-canada-mexico-usa-submit-bids-as-2026-world-cup-hosts |archive-date=September 8, 2017 |access-date=September 7, 2017}}</ref> A first-round elimination removed nine venues and nine cities. On June 16, 2022, the sixteen host cities–two in Canada, three in Mexico, and eleven in the United States–were announced by [[FIFA]]: Atlanta, Boston, Dallas, Guadalajara, Houston, Kansas City, Los Angeles, Mexico City, Miami, Monterrey, New York/New Jersey, Philadelphia, San Francisco Bay Area, Seattle, Toronto, and Vancouver.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.fifa.com/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/media-releases/media-release-greater-than-fwc-2026-greater-than-host-cities-announcement |title=FIFA unveils stellar line-up of FIFA World Cup 2026 Host Cities |publisher=FIFA |date=June 16, 2022|access-date=June 16, 2022|archive-date=June 16, 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220616223211/https://www.fifa.com/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/media-releases/media-release-greater-than-fwc-2026-greater-than-host-cities-announcement|url-status=live}}</ref> Eight of the sixteen chosen stadiums have permanent [[artificial turf]] surfaces that were replaced with grass under the direction of FIFA and a [[University of Tennessee]]–[[Michigan State University]] research team. Depending on the venue's climate, the turf used is either a hybrid of 84% [[Poa pratensis|Kentucky bluegrass]] and 16% [[Lolium perenne|perennial ryegrass]] (for cooler temperatures), or [[Cynodon dactylon|Bermuda grass]] (for warmer temperatures).<ref name="utk-fifa">{{cite web |title=Bringing UT's Turfgrass Expertise to FIFA World Cup 26 |url=https://www.utk.edu/turfgrass/ |publisher=UT Knoxville |access-date=April 25, 2026 |archive-date=April 21, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260421133616/https://www.utk.edu/turfgrass/ |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="tsn-henry-26">{{cite web |last1=Henry |first1=Genevieve |title=MSU Turf Management Program provides grass for World Cup |url=https://statenews.com/article/2026/04/msu-turf-management-program-provides-grass-for-world-cup?ct=content_open&cv=cbox_featured |publisher=The State News |access-date=April 25, 2026 |date=April 22, 2026 |archive-date=April 26, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260426022726/https://statenews.com/article/2026/04/msu-turf-management-program-provides-grass-for-world-cup%3Fct%3Dcontent_open%26cv%3Dcbox_featured |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="conv-tr-g-s-b-26">{{cite web |last1=Trey Rogers |first1=John N. |last2=Guevara |first2=Jackie Lyn A. |last3=Sorochan |first3=John |last4=Bearss |first4=Ryan |title=We designed the turf for soccer's biggest World Cup ever – here's how we created the same playing experience across 3 countries |url=https://theconversation.com/we-designed-the-turf-for-soccers-biggest-world-cup-ever-heres-how-we-created-the-same-playing-experience-across-3-countries-279536 |publisher=The Conversation |access-date=April 25, 2026 |date=April 20, 2026 |archive-date=April 25, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260425060702/https://theconversation.com/we-designed-the-turf-for-soccers-biggest-world-cup-ever-heres-how-we-created-the-same-playing-experience-across-3-countries-279536 |url-status=live }}</ref>

Four venues (Atlanta, Dallas, Houston, and Vancouver) are indoor stadiums that use [[retractable roof]] systems, all equipped with climate control, while a fifth, Los Angeles, is open-air but has a translucent roof and no climate control.<ref>{{cite news |last=Tannenwald |first=Jonathan |date=November 2, 2022 |title=FIFA goes to college to study how to grow grass indoors for the 2026 men's World Cup |work=[[The Philadelphia Inquirer]] |url=https://www.inquirer.com/soccer/2026-world-cup-stadiums-grass-john-sorochan-20221102.html |url-access=subscription |access-date=November 6, 2022 |archive-date=November 7, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221107071610/https://www.inquirer.com/soccer/2026-world-cup-stadiums-grass-john-sorochan-20221102.html |url-status=live}}</ref> The host of the [[2026 FIFA World Cup final|final match]]—[[MetLife Stadium]] in [[East Rutherford, New Jersey]]—was announced by FIFA on February 4, 2024.<ref name="ESPN-Schedule">{{cite news |last=Bonagura |first=Kyle |date=February 4, 2024 |title=2026 World Cup final set for MetLife Stadium, USMNT kicks off in L.A. |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/story/_/id/39437198/2026-world-cup-final-metlife-stadium-usa-kicks-la |publisher=ESPN |access-date=February 4, 2024 |archive-date=February 4, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240204211650/https://www.espn.com/soccer/story/_/id/39437198/2026-world-cup-final-metlife-stadium-usa-kicks-la |url-status=live}}</ref>

Although there are [[soccer-specific stadium]]s in Canada and the United States, the largest dedicated soccer-specific stadium in the United States, [[Geodis Park]] in [[Nashville, Tennessee]], seats 30,000, which falls short of FIFA's minimum of 40,000 (Toronto's [[BMO Field]] was expanded from 30,000 to 45,500 for this tournament).<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/toronto-fc-to-expand-bmo-field-to-host-2026-world-cup-matches |title=Toronto FC to expand BMO Field to host 2026 World Cup matches |last=Cattry |first=Pardeep |publisher=Major League Soccer |date=April 22, 2021|access-date=November 24, 2022|archive-date=November 24, 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221124015402/https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/toronto-fc-to-expand-bmo-field-to-host-2026-world-cup-matches|url-status=live}}</ref> Stadiums including [[Mercedes-Benz Stadium]] in [[Atlanta]]; [[Gillette Stadium]] in [[Foxborough, Massachusetts]]; and [[Lumen Field]] in [[Seattle]] are used by both [[National Football League]] (NFL) and [[Major League Soccer]] (MLS) teams.<ref>{{cite news |date=June 16, 2022 |title=World Cup 2026 host cities confirmed: What you need to know about the 16 venues |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/fifa-world-cup/story/4675639/world-cup-2026-host-cities-confirmed-what-you-need-to-know-about-the-16-venues |publisher=ESPN |access-date=November 6, 2022 |archive-date=November 7, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221107072053/https://www.espn.com/soccer/fifa-world-cup/story/4675639/world-cup-2026-host-cities-confirmed-what-you-need-to-know-about-the-16-venues |url-status=live}}</ref> Although all of the Canadian and American stadiums are primarily used for [[gridiron football]], with the American stadiums hosting NFL teams and Canada's hosting the [[Canadian Football League]] (CFL), they have been used on numerous occasions for soccer and are also designed to host that sport.<ref>{{cite news |last=Jones |first=J. [[Mexico City]] is the only capital of the three host nations chosen as a venue site; [[Ottawa]] and [[Washington, D.C.]], joined [[Bonn]] ([[1974 FIFA World Cup|West Germany, 1974]]) and [[Tokyo]] ([[2002 FIFA World Cup|Japan, 2002]]) as the only capital cities not selected to host World Cup matches. Washington was a host city candidate, but the poor state of [[Northwest Stadium]] caused the city to combine its bid with nearby [[Baltimore]]'s [[M&T Bank Stadium]], which was unsuccessful. Other cities eliminated from the final hosting list were [[Cincinnati]], [[Denver]], [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]], [[Orlando, Florida|Orlando]], and [[Edmonton]]. Ottawa's candidate venue, [[TD Place Stadium]], was eliminated early on for insufficient capacity.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Platt |first=Oliver |date=September 7, 2017 |title=Rogers Centre removed from list of potential World Cup 2026 venues |url=https://wakingthered.com/2017/09/07/1986/rogers-centre-removed-from-list-of-potential-world-cup-2026-venues/ |access-date=October 21, 2025 |website=Waking The Red |quote=Mosaic Stadium and TD Place Stadium do not currently meet the minimum-capacity requirement |archive-date=November 2, 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20251102222909/https://wakingthered.com/2017/09/07/1986/rogers-centre-removed-from-list-of-potential-world-cup-2026-venues/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Though eight of the metropolitan areas involved had previously hosted World Cup matches ([[Dallas–Fort Worth metroplex|Dallas]], [[Greater Los Angeles|Los Angeles]], [[San Francisco Bay Area]], [[New York metropolitan area|New York/New Jersey]], and [[Greater Boston|Boston]] in [[1994 FIFA World Cup|1994]]; [[Guadalajara metropolitan area|Guadalajara]] and [[Greater Mexico City|Mexico City]] in both [[1970 FIFA World Cup|1970]] and [[1986 FIFA World Cup|1986]]; [[Monterrey metropolitan area|Monterrey]] in 1986), [[Estadio Azteca]] is the only stadium in this tournament that had previously been used for a World Cup, in both 1970 and 1986; none of the stadiums used in the 1994 FIFA World Cup were used in this tournament (though MetLife Stadium is located at the same site as one of the 1994 venues, [[Giants Stadium]]).<ref>{{cite news |first1=Chuck |last1=Booth |first2=Roger |last2=Gonzalez |title=FIFA World Cup 2026 host cities: Los Angeles, New York/New Jersey among top venues; Two of the teams were seeded based on the [[FIFA Men's World Ranking|World Rankings]], and they played the winners of two knockout matches between the four unseeded teams for the two [[FIFA World Cup]] berths. The four-match tournament was played in Mexico, one of the host countries, and was also used as a test event for the FIFA World Cup.<ref name="slot" />

The ratification of slot allocation also gave the [[Oceania Football Confederation|OFC]] a guaranteed berth in the final tournament for the first time: the 2026 FIFA World Cup was the first tournament in which all six confederations have at least one guaranteed berth and also the first time since the [[2010 FIFA World Cup|2010 edition]] in which all confederations had a team qualify for the World Cup finals.<ref name="slot" />

The 48 teams were divided into four pots of 12. Pot 1 consisted of the three hosts and the top nine teams from the November 2025 [[FIFA Men's World Ranking]]. Pots 2, 3, and 4 consisted of the remaining teams according to the ranking. The four winners of the [[2026 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA second round|UEFA playoffs]] and the two winners of the inter-confederation playoffs were not known at the time of the draw and thus were automatically allocated to Pot 4. The 12 groups were randomly formed by selecting one team from each of the four pots. With the exception of UEFA, no group could have more than one team from the same confederation drawn into it.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://inside.fifa.com/organisation/media-releases/procedures-final-draw-world-cup-2026-revealed |title=Procedures for the Final Draw for the FIFA World Cup 2026 revealed |publisher=FIFA |date=November 25, 2025 |access-date=November 25, 2025 |archive-date=November 25, 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20251125181355/https://inside.fifa.com/organisation/media-releases/procedures-final-draw-world-cup-2026-revealed |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/2d1a1ac7bab78995/original/Draw-Procedures-for-the-FIFA-World-Cup-2026.pdf |title=Draw Procedures for the FIFA World Cup 2026 |publisher=FIFA |date=November 25, 2025 |access-date=November 25, 2025 |archive-date=January 15, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260115025032/https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/2d1a1ac7bab78995/original/Draw-Procedures-for-the-FIFA-World-Cup-2026.pdf |url-status=live }}</ref>

The three host nations were pre-allocated to three groups for scheduling purposes. Mexico was placed in Group A, Canada in Group B, and the United States in Group D.<ref name="Schedule">{{cite web |url=https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/1be9ce37eb98fcc5/original/FWC26-Match-Schedule_English.pdf |title=FIFA World Cup 26 – Match Schedule |publisher=FIFA |date=February 4, 2024 |access-date=February 4, 2024 |archive-date=February 5, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240205034420/https://digitalhub.fifa.com/m/1be9ce37eb98fcc5/original/FWC26-Match-Schedule_English.pdf |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last1=Bushnell |first1=Henry |last2=Crafton |first2=Adam |date=August 22, 2025 |title=2026 World Cup draw to be held at Kennedy Center, President Trump announces |url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/6569842/2025/08/22/world-cup-draw-kennedy-center-trump-infantino/ |access-date=August 22, 2025 |work=The New York Times |issn=0362-4331 |url-access=limited |archive-date=September 9, 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250909045259/https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/6569842/2025/08/22/world-cup-draw-kennedy-center-trump-infantino/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

The confederation restriction applied to all three potential winners of the inter-confederation playoffs. FIFA also announced that, "in the interest of ensuring competitive balance", the teams ranked first (Spain), second (Argentina), third (France), and fourth (England) would be drawn into opposite pathways. Therefore, should these teams win their groups, they would be unable to meet until the semifinals.<ref>{{cite web |last=Johnson |first=Dale |date=November 25, 2025 |title=Fifa to keep top seeds apart in World Cup draw |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/articles/cvg84knn2p5o |access-date=November 25, 2025 |work=[[BBC Sport]] |archive-date=December 18, 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20251218130250/https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/articles/cvg84knn2p5o |url-status=live }}</ref> The draw started with Pot 1 and ended with Pot 4, with each team selected and then allocated into the first available group alphabetically. For the purpose of the match schedule, the Pot 1 teams were automatically drawn into position 1 of each group. For the remaining pots, FIFA established a predetermined pattern to define the position of teams based on their pot and the group they were drawn into.

===Squads===
{{Main|2026 FIFA World Cup squads}}

Before submitting their final squad for the tournament, teams named a provisional squad of between 35 and 55 players one month prior to the tournament. Teams were required to name their final squads by June 2. If a player becomes too injured or ill to participate in the tournament, he can be replaced by another player from the provisional squad until 24 hours before the team's first match. However, an injured or ill goalkeeper may be replaced by another goalkeeper from the provisional squad at any time during the tournament.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/squad-lists-number-date |title=When are World Cup squads named, and how many players will feature? |publisher=[[FIFA]] |date=May 6, 2026 |access-date=May 7, 2026 |archive-date=May 12, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260512015857/https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/squad-lists-number-date |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Knockout stage==
{{Main|2026 FIFA World Cup knockout stage}}

The knockout stage uses a [[Single-elimination tournament|single-elimination format]], starting with the newly introduced round of 32 on June 28 and culminating with the [[2026 FIFA World Cup final|final]] on July 19. On the day prior, a [[#Match for third place|match for third place]] will also be played. In the knockout stage, if the scores are level when normal playing time expires, 30 minutes of [[Overtime (sports)#Association football|extra time]] will be played. If still tied at the end of extra time, a [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shootout]] is used to determine which team wins the match.<ref name="regulations" />

===Discipline===
A player or team official is automatically suspended for the next match of the tournament for the following offenses:<ref name="regulations" />
* Receiving a red card (red card suspensions may be extended for serious offenses).
* Receiving two yellow cards in the tournament.{{efn-ua|As yellow cards are not carried forward to [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shootouts]], players may be shown two yellow cards in the same match without being sent off. However, this would result in a suspension for accumulating two yellow cards during the tournament.}} This yellow card count is reset after the completion of the group stage and again after the quarterfinals.<ref name="FIFA Council April 28">{{cite web |url=https://inside.fifa.com/organisation/fifa-council/media-releases/council-increases-record-financial-distribution-member-associations-world-cup-2026 |title=FIFA Council increases record financial distribution to all 48 Participating Member Associations at the FIFA World Cup 2026 |publisher=[[FIFA]] |date=April 28, 2026 |access-date=April 28, 2026}}</ref> Resets do not apply to suspensions; a player whose yellow card count reaches two in the match immediately before a reset is still suspended for the next match.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2026-06-15 |title=2026 FIFA World Cup yellow card and suspension rules |url=https://www.mlssoccer.com/competitions/fifa-world-cup/news/2026-fifa-world-cup-yellow-card-and-suspension-rules |access-date=2026-06-29 |website=MLS Soccer |language=en}}</ref>

During qualification, [[Cristiano Ronaldo]] was sent off for violent conduct in Portugal's penultimate match against the [[Republic of Ireland national football team|Republic of Ireland]], with such an offense typically resulting in a ban of at least two matches. Ronaldo was handed a three-match ban, though the final two matches of the ban were suspended for a one-year probationary period, making him eligible to appear in Portugal's opening World Cup match.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Cristiano Ronaldo: Portugal captain avoids ban for start of nation's 2026 World Cup campaign |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/13475321/cristiano-ronaldo-portugal-captain-avoids-ban-for-start-of-nations-2026-world-cup-campaign |access-date=November 25, 2025 |website=Sky Sports}}</ref> On May 8, 2026, the Bureau of the [[FIFA Council]] amended the tournament regulations so that pending one- or two-match suspensions resulting from red cards for two yellow cards, denying an obvious goal-scoring opportunity, or serious foul play during qualification would no longer be carried forward to the final competition.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://inside.fifa.com/organisation/fifa-council/news/council-update-regulations-world-cup-2026 |title=Bureau of the Council update on the Regulations for the FIFA World Cup 2026 |publisher=[[FIFA]] |date=May 8, 2026 |access-date=May 8, 2026}}</ref> This exempted Argentina's [[Nicolás Otamendi]], Ecuador's [[Moisés Caicedo]], and Qatar's [[Tarek Salman]] from serving their qualifying-round suspensions during the tournament, with the bans to instead be served in a subsequent competition.<ref>{{cite web |last=Dunbar |first=Graham |url=https://apnews.com/article/world-cup-fifa-ban-otamendi-caicedo-196ea65dff44d19b43d7e0835fa42398 |title=FIFA waives one-game bans for Otamendi and Caicedo at World Cup after being sent off in qualifier |work=[[Associated Press]] |date=May 8, 2026 |access-date=May 8, 2026}}</ref>

===Prize money===
In April 2026, FIFA confirmed the prizes for all participating nations, with a total distribution for the expanded tournament of $871&nbsp;million, which is nearly double the prize pool of the previous tournament. Performance-based prizes amount to $671&nbsp;million of the $871&nbsp;million total, with the remaining $200&nbsp;million paid out among teams regardless of performance. Each qualified team received preparation money in the amount of $2.5&nbsp;million; combining this with an elimination in the group stage, the minimum total amount received per team is $12.5&nbsp;million, with the winner receiving $52.5&nbsp;million.<ref>{{Cite web |title=FIFA Council increases record financial distribution for FIFA World Cup 2026 |url=https://inside.fifa.com/organisation/fifa-council/media-releases/council-increases-record-financial-distribution-member-associations-world-cup-2026 |publisher=FIFA |access-date=May 3, 2026 |archive-date=April 30, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260430102144/https://inside.fifa.com/organisation/fifa-council/media-releases/council-increases-record-financial-distribution-member-associations-world-cup-2026 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=2026 FIFA World Cup Prize Money: How Much Will the Champions Really Earn? |url=https://worldcuplocaltime.com/2026-fifa-world-cup-prize-money/ |website=World Cup Local Time |access-date=May 3, 2026 |date=May 3, 2026 |last=Sharma |first=Pooja |orig-date=January 25, 2026 |archive-date=June 3, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260603200753/https://worldcuplocaltime.com/2026-fifa-world-cup-prize-money/ |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=June 24, 2026 |title=World Cup 2026: What is the prize money? |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/articles/c9821x9lpdjo |access-date=June 24, 2026 |website=BBC Sport |language=en-GB |archive-date=June 25, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260625022652/https://www.bbc.com/sport/articles/c9821x9lpdjo |url-status=live }}</ref>

===Individual and team awards===
{{See also|FIFA World Cup awards}}

The following awards are to be presented at the end of the tournament.<ref>{{Cite news |title=What are the World Cup award names?  {{!}} Britannica |url=https://www.britannica.com/question/What-are-the-World-Cup-award-names |access-date=June 23, 2026 |work=Encyclopedia Britannica |language=en |archive-date=June 23, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260623230040/https://www.britannica.com/question/What-are-the-World-Cup-award-names |url-status=live }}</ref>

*Golden Boot: Awarded to the tournament's top goal scorer.
*Golden Glove: Awarded to the tournament's best goalkeeper.
*Golden Ball: Awarded to the best overall player of the tournament.
*FIFA Young Player Award: Awarded to the best overall player of the tournament under the age of 21.
*FIFA Fair Play Trophy: Awarded to the team with the best disciplinary record that reached the knockout stage.

Additionally, a "Superior Player of the Match" award is presented after each fixture to the player deemed to have delivered the most outstanding performance and greatest impact in that match. The recipient is selected through a public online vote conducted by FIFA. The award is sponsored by [[Michelob Ultra]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/michelob-ultra-superior-player-of-match-winner |title=Michelob Ultra Superior Player of the Match - every winner |website=FIFA |date=June 11, 2026 |language=en |access-date=June 15, 2026 |archive-date=June 16, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260616042227/https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/michelob-ultra-superior-player-of-match-winner |url-status=live }}</ref> Muslim players, such as [[Mohamed Salah]] and [[Lamine Yamal]], received a brandless trophy, out of respect to [[Khamr|views on alcohol in Islam]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Diario Sport |date=2026-07-03 |title=El MVP ya tiene dueño: Lamine Yamal |url=https://www.sport.es/es/videos/mundial-futbol/20260703/mvp-dueno-lamine-yamal/132070513.shtml |access-date=2026-07-03 |website=Diario Sport |language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Serrano |first=Carlos |date=2026-06-26 |title=La FIFA cambia el trofeo MVP del partido a los musulmanes: retira el logo de la cerveza patrocinadora del Mundial |url=https://www.elespanol.com/deportes/futbol/mundial/20260626/fifa-cambia-trofeo-mvp-partido-musulmanes-retira-logo-cerveza-patrocinadora-mundial/1003744301930_0.html |access-date=2026-07-03 |website=El Español |language=es}}</ref>

==Marketing==

===Branding===
[[File:2026 FIFA WC countdown clock Paseo de la Reforma.jpg|thumb|Countdown clock on [[Paseo de la Reforma]], Mexico City in September 2025)|alt=A freestanding digital clock with Fifa branding placed on the sidewalk.]]

The official emblem and brand identity was unveiled on May 17, 2023, at the [[Griffith Observatory]] in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]]; its basic form consists of a stacked "26" with an image of the [[FIFA World Cup Trophy]] in front of it, designed to be adaptable to different backdrops. ===Tickets===
{{See also|List of 2026 FIFA World Cup controversies#Ticket prices}}

Ticket prices for the 2026 FIFA World Cup initially ranged from $60 for group stage matches to $6,730 for the final—largely increased from the USD equivalent of $69 to $1,607 in the [[2022 FIFA World Cup]]. However, in September 2025, FIFA confirmed it would use [[dynamic pricing]] for tickets for the first time, following the practice used in the [[2025 FIFA Club World Cup]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |date=September 3, 2025 |title=FIFA to use dynamic pricing for World Cup tickets |url=https://www.espn.com/soccer/story/_/id/46147005/2026-world-cup-tickets-fifa-dynamic-pricing-prices |access-date=September 3, 2025 |website=ESPN.com |archive-date=September 3, 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250903231526/https://www.espn.com/soccer/story/_/id/46147005/2026-world-cup-tickets-fifa-dynamic-pricing-prices |url-status=live }}</ref> Hospitality seats were made available in April 2025 via FIFA's ticket partner for the event.<ref>{{Cite web |date=May 6, 2025 |title=Limited initial release of FIFA World Cup 26 hospitality packages for matches in United States launched |url=https://inside.fifa.com/tournament-organisation/commercial/news/limited-initial-release-of-fifa-world-cup-26-tm-hospitality-packages-for |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250714204258/https://inside.fifa.com/tournament-organisation/commercial/news/limited-initial-release-of-fifa-world-cup-26-tm-hospitality-packages-for |archive-date=July 14, 2025 |access-date=May 20, 2026 |website=FIFA |url-status=live }}</ref>

An initial draw period for non-hospitality seats occurred between September 10–19, 2025, limited to [[Visa Inc.|Visa]] cardholders. A second phase ran from October 27–31, 2025, and a third phase started after the final draw of teams on December 5. Sales are capped at four tickets per person per match, and no person is able to purchase more than 40 tickets for the overall tournament. FIFA's official resale platform went live on October 2, 2025.<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web |date=September 30, 2025 |title=FIFA Resale/Exchange Marketplace |url=https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/resale-ticket-exchange-marketplace |access-date=May 20, 2026 |website=FIFA}}</ref>

A final "last-minute" sales phase reopened on April 22, 2026, approximately 50 days before the start of the tournament, with tickets for all 104 matches made available on a first-come, first-served basis. By that stage, more than five million tickets had been sold out of an expected total exceeding six million, with additional tickets scheduled to be released in phases up to the final, subject to availability.<ref name="Reuters042226">{{cite news |title=World Cup last-minute ticket sales phase re-opens 50 days from kick-off |work=Reuters |date=April 22, 2026 |url=https://www.reuters.com/sports/soccer/world-cup-last-minute-ticket-sales-phase-re-opens-50-days-kick-off-2026-04-22/ |access-date=April 27, 2026}}</ref>

Every city hosting the World Cup in the United States has passed a law stating that ticket sales to World Cup events are exempt from state and local sales taxes.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://revisor.mo.gov/main/OneSection.aspx?section=144.051&bid=51142|title=144.051|website=revisor.mo.gov}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Wilson |first=Jonathan |date=April 2, 2026 |title=More than half of World Cup countries face extra costs as Fifa fails to agree US tax deal |url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2026/apr/02/world-cup-countries-face-extra-costs-fifa-tax-deal-us-government |work=The Guardian |access-date=April 2, 2026}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Gray |first=Page |date=December 5, 2025 |title=Not-So-Free Kick: How the 2026 FIFA World Cup Will Cost Cities Millions |url=https://itep.org/fifa-2026-world-cup-tickets-sales-tax-exemption/ |work=Institute on Taxation and Economic Policy |access-date=April 2, 2026 |archive-date=April 6, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260406210735/https://itep.org/fifa-2026-world-cup-tickets-sales-tax-exemption/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

===Attendance===
The tournament set the record for the highest cumulative attendance in World Cup history. On June 25, 2026, during the [[2026 FIFA World Cup Group E#Ecuador vs Germany|Group E match between Ecuador and Germany]] at MetLife Stadium, total attendance reached 3,605,357 spectators. This surpassed the previous record of 3,587,538,<ref name="USATodayRecord2026">{{cite news |last1=D'Andrea |first1=Christian |title=World Cup attendance record shattered before group stage even ends |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/soccer/worldcup/2026/06/25/world-cup-attendance-record-2026-1994-united-states/90701931007/ |work=[[USA Today]] |date=June 25, 2026 |access-date=June 27, 2026 |archive-date=June 27, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260627014340/https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/soccer/worldcup/2026/06/25/world-cup-attendance-record-2026-1994-united-states/90701931007/ |url-status=live }}</ref> which had been held for 32 years by the 1994 FIFA World Cup, also hosted in the United States. The record was broken with 44 matches remaining in the tournament.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Florio |first1=Mike |title=With 44 matches to go, the 2026 World Cup has already set an all-time attendance record |url=https://www.nbcsports.com/nfl/profootballtalk/rumor-mill/news/with-44-matches-to-go-the-2026-world-cup-as-already-set-an-all-time-attendance-record |work=[[NBC Sports]] |date=June 26, 2026 |access-date=June 27, 2026 |archive-date=June 26, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260626193917/https://www.nbcsports.com/nfl/profootballtalk/rumor-mill/news/with-44-matches-to-go-the-2026-world-cup-as-already-set-an-all-time-attendance-record |url-status=live }}</ref> Across the first 60 matches, venues operated at an average occupancy rate of 99.7%.<ref>{{cite news |title=WC 2026: World Cup sets attendance records already in the group stage! |url=https://stadiumdb.com/news/2026/06/wc_2026_world_cup_sets_attendance_records_already_in_the_group_stage |work=StadiumDB |date=June 26, 2026 |access-date=June 27, 2026}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Bonomo |first1=Griffin |title=Ecuador, Germany at MetLife Stadium helps 2026 World Cup set all-time attendance record |url=https://www.amny.com/sports/world-cup-attendance-record-all-time-6-25-26/ |work=amNewYork |date=June 26, 2026 |access-date=June 27, 2026 |archive-date=June 27, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260627003045/https://www.amny.com/sports/world-cup-attendance-record-all-time-6-25-26/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Additionally, a single-day World Cup attendance record was established on June 16, 2026, when 281,223 fans attended four group stage matches.<ref>{{cite web |title=FIFA World Cup 2026™ sets new daily attendance record |url=https://inside.fifa.com/news/world-cup-2026-sets-new-daily-attendance-record |publisher=[[FIFA]] |date=June 16, 2026 |access-date=June 27, 2026}}</ref>

===Match ball===
{{Main|Adidas Trionda}}
{{See also|List of FIFA World Cup official match balls}}
[[File:FIFA World Cup 2026 Draw Reception (54972841852).jpg|thumb|[[Adidas Trionda]], the match ball|242x242px|alt=The match ball displayed on a stand.]]

On May 2, 2025, reports surfaced that the match ball would be called [[Adidas Trionda]]. The design features red, green, and blue (the three colors representing Canada, Mexico, and the United States, respectively, and also featured on the host countries' flags), as well as a white wave connecting each of the colors, hence the name using the [[Spanish language|Spanish]] words for three (tri) and wave (onda).<ref>{{Cite web |last=Steiner |first=Ben |date=May 2, 2025 |title=FIFA World Cup 2026 'Trionda' Match Ball Leaks, Featuring U.S., Mexico and Canada Colors |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/fifa-world-cup-2026-trionda-match-ball-leak |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250502232842/https://www.si.com/soccer/fifa-world-cup-2026-trionda-match-ball-leak |archive-date=May 2, 2025 |magazine=Sports Illustrated}}</ref> The design also features the national symbols of the three host countries (a [[maple leaf]] for Canada, a [[Golden eagle#canadensis|golden eagle]] for Mexico, and a [[five-pointed star]] for the United States) as well as gold embellishments to represent the [[FIFA World Cup Trophy|World Cup Trophy]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=October 2, 2025 |title=FIFA celebrates launch of Official Match Ball of FIFA World Cup 26™: TRIONDA |url=https://inside.fifa.com/organisation/media-releases/fifa-celebrates-launch-official-match-ball-world-cup-26-trionda |access-date=October 2, 2025 |website=FIFA |archive-date=November 6, 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20251106234307/https://inside.fifa.com/organisation/media-releases/fifa-celebrates-launch-official-match-ball-world-cup-26-trionda |url-status=live }}</ref>

On June 11, 2025, and in preparation for the 2026 FIFA World Cup, [[NASA]] conducted experiments aboard the International Space Station using soccer balls to study how mass distribution and embedded sensor technology affect ball motion and stability in microgravity. According to NASA, the research contributed to the development and evaluation of technologies used in the 2026 FIFA World Cup official match ball.<ref>{{cite web |title=Soccer Meets Space Science |url=https://www.nasa.gov/image-article/soccer-meets-space-science/ |publisher=NASA |access-date=June 20, 2026 |archive-date=June 18, 2026 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20260618144546/https://www.nasa.gov/image-article/soccer-meets-space-science/ |url-status=live }}</ref>
